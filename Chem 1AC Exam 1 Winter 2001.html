EXAM 1

 

1.         The members of team “Cougars” built a good calorimeter. To calibrate it, they measured 100.0 mL of methanol (CH3OH) at 15.0 ºC and placed it inside the calorimeter. Then they added 100 mL of methanol at 40 ºC, and the temperature rose to 25  ºC.

a.      Calculate the value of Ccal for their calorimeter.

b.      Compare the value with the value you obtained for your calorimeter and explain any difference you could find.
Data: dmethanol = 0.791 g cm-3     Cp methanol = 2.55 J g-1 K-1


2.         The “Cougars” wanted to use their calorimeter to measure the enthalpy of the reaction of uranium and oxygen to form uranium dioxide UO2, but the experimental difficulties and the hazards involved were too high, so their instructor suggested that they use the data of the following reactions to calculate the desired value:

3UO2(s) + O2(g)  ® U3O8(s)                             ΔH = -318.0 kJ mol-1
3U(s) + 4O2(g) ® U3O8(s)                                ΔH = -3571 kJ mol-1


3.         In ordinary physical an chemical research, scientist normally use a bomb calorimeter. In this instrument, reactions occur inside a metallic vessel hermetically closed. The heat evolved in the reaction inside the bomb is measured by the raise in the temperature of the water bath surrounding the bomb. Explain the difference in using the values obtained in your calorimeter when calculating the enthalpy change of a chemical reaction.
