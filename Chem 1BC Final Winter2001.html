1.         Team “Apples” wanted to measure the heat of dissolution of ammonium chloride. They placed 0.5549 g of the salt in a calorimeter that contained 100 mL of water at 22.0 °C. The final temperature in the calorimeter was 20.8 °C. Their previously determined calorimeter constant was 20 J K‑1.

a.      Calculate the experimental heat of dissolution of ammonium chloride.

b.      What was the percent error of their determination?

 

2.          Team “Oranges” constructed a galvanic cell in which they put a copper electrode in a solution of 0.1 mol L‑1 Cu2+(aq) and a zinc electrode in 0.5 mol L‑1 Zn2+(aq)

a.      Calculate the potential of the cell when they connected the electrodes

b.      Calculate the potential of the cell after 1.000 g of Cu have been deposited on the cathode. Assume the volume of each cell as 1.0 L.
